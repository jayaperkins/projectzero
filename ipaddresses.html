Info

How can a random set of numbers enable computers to communicate? Why are they important? What privacy measures should be taken to prevent security risks associatd with them?

Every one of these questions should be answered in order to properly understand the importance of this system.

IP addresses should be treated like home addresses. Don't give your IP address to people you would not trust. In fact, don't even provide it to anyone. Security concerns can be avoided by keeping your personal IP a secret. Let the computers deal with the numbers, not humans.

IP addresses enable computers to "talk" to each other. Each device has a unique number that provides its identification to the world. IP stands for "Internet Protocol." This is the standardized form for computer communication.

IP addresses also enable browsers to determine approximate locations to provide relevant information to users.

Clearly, IP addresses are one of the greatest inventions in the history of modern computers.
